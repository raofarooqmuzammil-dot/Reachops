FAQ

Common Questions

What are your coverage hours?
+
Core services (Dispatch, Scheduling, Customer Service) run 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM Monday through Friday. After-hours and weekend coverage is available at $7.00/hour (standard) or $10.50/hour (holidays). You choose the window and we staff accordingly.
Are there any setup fees?
+
None. $0.00 setup fee. We provide complimentary onboarding, software integration, and driver training to ensure a seamless transition from day one. You pay nothing until your service begins.
What is the contract length? Can I cancel?
+
All agreements are based on a 12-month recurring term. Either party may terminate with a 30-day written notice. We believe in earning your business every month, which is why our cancellation policy is straightforward and fair.
What's the difference between a Dedicated and Shared Dispatcher?
+
With a Shared Team, your fleet is managed by our professional pool of agents who handle multiple clients efficiently. A Dedicated Dispatcher is exclusively assigned to your fleet — they learn your routes, drivers, and preferences deeply. Dedicated starts at $3,000+/month and is quoted based on fleet size.
Do you offer after-hours and weekend coverage for NEMT?
+
Yes. After-hours billing is calculated on the total hours of your requested coverage window and added to your monthly base total. Standard rate is $7/hr, holiday rate is $10.50/hr. Just let us know your schedule and we'll staff accordingly.
Can I combine NEMT services with marketing or SEO?
+
Absolutely. Many of our clients use ReachOps as their single operations partner — handling dispatch during the day while our marketing team builds their brand and local SEO presence. We can bundle services and create a custom package that works for your budget.
How quickly can you get started?
+
Typically within 48 hours of signing. We conduct a discovery call, gather your tools and access, complete onboarding, and have your team operational. No drawn-out implementation. We move fast because your business needs are immediate.
